07 — The ADHD Protocol

How to not blow up your own progress

You called it out yourself. ADHD has been your biggest enemy with this setup — wanting to build 10 things at once, not verifying, breaking everything. Here's the operating system that works with how your brain runs.

🧠 The 5 Rules That Will Save You From Yourself

1
Brain dump first, build second. When you have 10 ideas, dump them all to your agent in one message. Say "log all of these and we'll come back." Then pick ONE. Work that one until it's done and verified. The others will be there — they're logged.
2
The "Verify Before Next" rule. Your agent told you this and you know it's true: you gave it 10 features, didn't verify any of them, and broke everything. One feature. Test it. Works? Next feature. Doesn't work? Fix it before moving on. No exceptions.
3
Never start a build after 10pm. You're up at 5–6am every day grinding. The late-night build sessions when you're tired are where configs get broken and rabbit holes start. After 10pm: planning and reading only. No config changes.
4
Before sleeping: set 3 tasks, no more. Tell your agent exactly what to do overnight, with clear success criteria. "Build X, test it does Y, log result to Z." Three things. If it can't do those three things reliably, it can't do ten.
5
Use this report as your queue. When the ADHD urge hits to go build something new, come back to this document. If it's not in Priorities 1–5 in order, it goes to a backlog list. Not now. After the current priority is done.
