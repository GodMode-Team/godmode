04 — Daily Intelligence

Priority 2: Your Daily Intelligence Brief

Every morning, instead of opening email and reacting — your AI pushes you a brief. What came in overnight. What's on your calendar. Your top 3 priorities. Anything that could blow up. You start in control, not reactive. Run it manually for one week, then automate it as a 6:30am cron to Telegram.

⚙️
To automate In OpenClaw, type /cron → create a daily job at 6:30am → paste the prompt below. It pushes your brief to Telegram before you're out of bed. 
Run my daily intelligence brief. Here's what I need:

1. OVERNIGHT SUMMARY
Scan my email and Slack from the last 18 hours. Pull out anything that:
- Requires a decision or response from me today
- Is time-sensitive (someone waiting on me, a deadline approaching)
- Is unusual, unexpected, or escalated from normal

Ignore: newsletters, marketing, automated notifications, receipts. Flag anything from my direct reports, key vendors, or clients as higher priority.

2. TODAY'S SCHEDULE
Pull my calendar for today. For each meeting, tell me:
- What it is and who's in it
- Any prep I should do (based on context you know about this person/topic)
- Flag any conflicts or back-to-backs I should know about

3. TOP 3 PRIORITIES FOR TODAY
Based on my email, calendar, and anything I told you recently — give me the 3 things I absolutely must move forward today. Not a task list — the 3 things that, if I do nothing else, make today a win.

4. ANYTHING I SHOULD WATCH FOR
Flag anything that looks like it could blow up, needs a fast decision, or that I'm at risk of dropping. One or two sentences per item. No noise — only real flags.

Format this cleanly. Keep it tight. I want to read it in under 3 minutes.
✅
Start manually for one week Once it's consistently useful, set up the 6:30am cron. That's when it becomes a real system — shows up before you go looking for it.
📬
Phase 2 — Hourly Email Heartbeat After the morning brief is dialed in, add a second cron every hour during business hours. Quick push to Telegram: "here's what came in since your last check, here's what needs attention." No inbox diving. Just signal. This is the proactive filtering you described — staying aware without living in it. Set it up Week 2.
05 — Email Triage

Priority 3: Email Triage for You and Michelle

You were clear — you don't want autonomous email sending. You want the signal filtered and surfaced. That's exactly what this does. Categorizes everything, archives noise, hands you a clean action list with draft responses for review. You steer, it drafts, you send.

⚠️
Exchange Server Note You're on Microsoft 365 + Exchange Server on-premise, not Gmail. Standard OAuth email integration won't work out of the box. You'll need IMAP or Microsoft Graph API access enabled first. Flag this with your IT contact, or bring it to your follow-up call with Caleb — it's a one-time setup step. 
Run a full email triage on my inbox. Here's how I want you to work:

STEP 1 — CATEGORIZE
Go through the last 48 hours of email (or all unread). Sort every email into one of these buckets:
- 🔴 ACTION REQUIRED — needs my response or a decision, today
- 🟡 DELEGATE TO MICHELLE — she can handle or respond to this
- 🟢 FYI / ARCHIVE — no action needed, safe to archive
- ⚫ NOISE — newsletters, automated, marketing — archive immediately

STEP 2 — SURFACE THE REDS
For each 🔴 item:
- Who it's from and what they need
- Draft a response (3-5 sentences, direct, in my voice)
- How urgent: respond today or can it wait 24h?

STEP 3 — BRIEF MICHELLE
For each 🟡 item, one-line instruction:
Example: "Reply to [name] — confirm Tuesday appointment, ask for contract first."

STEP 4 — ARCHIVE REPORT
What you archived and why. One line each so I can spot-check.

RULES:
- Never commit me to something without flagging it
- Client, vendor, or lawyer = high priority always
- Anything that feels off — unusual request, angry tone, legal/financial risk — top of the list
- Drafts in my voice: direct, no fluff, get to the point
👩‍💼
For Michelle Have her run this at 8am and 2pm each day. Action list ready before she opens a single email manually. Within a week she'll be processing 2x the volume in half the time.
⚠️
Start read-only for two weeks OpenClaw drafts, you or Michelle reviews, you send. Once you're approving ~80% of drafts without changes, expand Michelle's authority. Don't skip the trust-building phase.
