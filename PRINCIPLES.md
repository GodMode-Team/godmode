# PRINCIPLES.md
*The beliefs that govern every decision.*

---

## 1. Sovereign by Default, Cloud by Choice

Your data lives on your machine, under your control — cloud is an option, never a requirement.

Most software is built for the vendor's convenience, not the user's security. GodMode starts from the opposite position: your thoughts, your context, your history belong to you. Local-first means you own the system when the internet is down, when a vendor goes under, when your requirements change. We integrate with cloud services when they add value. We never make them mandatory. Privacy isn't a checkbox we add at the end — it's an architecture decision we make at the beginning.

---

## 2. The Ally Manages Complexity

The user gives direction — everything else is handled.

Most AI tools make you do the work: write prompts, manage agents, debug pipelines, stitch together APIs, pick models. GodMode inverts this. The Ally is the single surface between you and a team of specialist agents. You don't configure anything. You don't chase tasks across tabs. You don't troubleshoot. The complexity lives in the engine, not on your desk. If a user has to debug something the system should have handled, we failed. Simplicity on the outside requires sophistication on the inside — that's the job.

---

## 3. Modularity is the Moat

New capabilities plug in as files — skills, personas, recipes — not code changes.

The most important architectural decision GodMode made is that the engine stays fixed and behavior is configurable. Any developer, any power user, any enterprise can extend the system without touching core code. This means the platform grows without becoming bloated, the ecosystem becomes the product, and when we get something wrong — we will — it can be corrected in a file, not a release. The engine stays lean. The surface area grows infinitely. That asymmetry is the moat.

---

## 4. Memory Compounds

Every interaction makes the system more useful — an AI that forgets is an assistant, an AI that remembers is a partner.

Day 40 is smarter than Day 1. Not because we retrained the model — because the system has been paying attention. GodMode tracks what matters: your priorities, your patterns, your people, your decisions, your contradictions. Memory is the foundation of trust. Every time GodMode has to ask you something it already knows, it has failed. Every time it connects a dot you didn't ask it to connect, it has earned something. Forgetting is the enemy of the product we're building.

---

## 5. Human-Led, AI-Executed

The human sets direction — AI executes. The user is always the operator, never the operated.

GodMode is a force multiplier, not a replacement. The AI's job is to take direction and deliver results — not to set priorities, not to make strategic calls, not to act without permission. This line exists for a reason. When AI starts making decisions without explicit human direction, the product has drifted somewhere dangerous. We draw that line clearly, we design the product around it, and we don't drift past it. Direction belongs to the human. Execution belongs to the agents.

---

## 6. Earn Trust Through Competence

The product proves itself through verified execution — no marketing claims, no hype, just results.

We don't ship announcements about how smart our AI is. We ship things that work, then let users feel the difference. When something breaks, we show our work — what failed, why, what changed. Trust is built in reps, not in copy. This applies to every agent output, every feature release, every benchmark we choose to share. The only credibility worth having is credibility you earned. Show the receipts. Let the work speak for itself.

---

## 7. The Soul is the Product

GodMode doesn't just know your tasks — it knows your mission, your values, your family, your blind spots, and the depth of that understanding IS the differentiation.

Every AI tool knows what you ask it. GodMode is built to know you. Not because we asked you to fill out a profile — because the system was paying attention. Your decision-making patterns, what you're avoiding, why this opportunity sounds exciting but feels wrong, what you need to hear versus what you want to hear. This depth isn't a feature. It's the entire reason GodMode exists. Anyone can build a task manager. Very few products can tell you what you're running from. That's the product.

---

## 8. Connect, Don't Replace

GodMode orchestrates the tools you already use — it never rebuilds what already exists.

The fastest way to lose users is to make them recreate their stack inside your product. GodMode doesn't have a CRM, an email client, a calendar app, a project board, or a note editor. It connects to the ones you already use and orchestrates across them. This keeps the engine lean, keeps the integrations deep, and respects that users have already made tool decisions. We don't compete with the ecosystem — we complete it. The Ally orchestrates. It doesn't displace.

---

## 9. Bias Toward Action

A plan that never executes is noise — GodMode defaults to doing.

When you're ready to move, the system moves with you. When something can be done without a meeting, it gets done. When a deliverable can be drafted while you sleep, it's waiting in your inbox. The product earns its place by closing the gap between intention and output. Deliberation is valuable when stakes are high — otherwise, it's procrastination with better vocabulary. Speed is a product value, not just a personality preference. Build for people who move fast.

---

## 10. The Socket Board

GodMode is the OS — every subsystem is a module with a standard interface that can be swapped, extended, or replaced.

Memory, task systems, model providers, mission control, dashboards — none of these are GodMode's job to own forever. GodMode's job is to make each layer pluggable. The default stack is carefully chosen and it works out of the box. But when a better memory system ships, users swap the module. When a team prefers ClickUp over the built-in task system, they plug it in. When Venice or Ollama fits a user's privacy requirements better than Anthropic, they configure it. The socket board doesn't lock anyone in — to our implementation, our vendor choices, or our defaults. This is what makes GodMode the last AI product you install: everything else plugs in.

---

*If a feature doesn't serve at least one of these principles, we don't build it.*
